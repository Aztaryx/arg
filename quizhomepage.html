Game Modes — Overview

A quick summary of what each mode is about and how it works.

⚡ Blitzkrieg

"Race the clock. Answer as many as you can."

  • You have 2 minutes and 30 seconds to answer as many questions as possible.
  • Questions are drawn from one of four tracks — choose before you start.
  • Each correct answer adds to your score; wrong answers don't subtract, but they waste time.
  • The clock doesn't stop between questions — keep moving.
  • Your final score is based on how many you got right before time ran out.

👁️ Paranoia

"Two questions. One is wrong. Don't let doubt consume you."

  • Each round presents two question cards — only one is the correct one to answer.
  • A Sanity Meter fills when you hesitate too long or answer incorrectly.
  • The game is divided into five Acts, each more intense than the last.
  • If your sanity hits zero, the game ends — regardless of which Act you're on.
  • Survive all five Acts to complete the mode.

💀 Sudden Death

"One wrong answer. Game over."

  • There is no timer and no meter — just you and the question.
  • Answer correctly and you move on. Answer wrong and the run is over — immediately.
  • Questions get progressively harder the deeper you go.
  • Your score is simply how many questions you answered correctly in a row.
  • Challenge yourself to beat your own streak.

🌊 Overload

"The board fills up. Clear it before it buries you."

  • Questions appear on a board and pile up over time — answer them to clear the slots.
  • If the board becomes completely full, the game ends.
  • New questions arrive at increasing speed as you progress.
  • Correct answers clear one slot; wrong answers keep the slot occupied.
  • Survive as long as possible and rack up the highest score you can.

🕶️ Blackout

"You had five seconds to read it. Hope you were paying attention."

  • A question is displayed for only five seconds — then it disappears.
  • You must answer from memory once the question is hidden.
  • No re-reading, no second looks. What you caught is all you get.
  • Wrong or too-slow answers count against your score.
  • Tests your short-term recall as much as your actual knowledge.
